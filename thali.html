The Tradition

What Is a Thali?

A Thali is one of India's oldest and most celebrated ways of eating — a large round platter arranged with small bowls (katoris) of different dishes, all served together. The word "thali" simply means plate in Hindi, but the experience is anything but simple.

Every element on the platter is intentional: a balance of flavors (spicy, sweet, sour, salty), textures (soft curries, crispy farsan, fluffy roti), and nutrients (proteins from dal and kathol, carbohydrates from rice, vitamins from vegetables). A proper Thali is a full meal — not a sampler.

At Vatan, our Thali is rooted in the Gujarati and Kathiyawadi tradition — fresh, lovingly prepared, and served the way it's been served across generations of Indian families.

Thali Questions

What is an Indian Thali?

A Thali is a traditional Indian meal served on a large round platter with multiple small dishes — curries, dal, rice, breads, pickles, chutneys, and sweets — representing a complete, balanced meal in one sitting.

Does Vatan offer a Jain or Swaminarayan Thali?

Yes. We offer both a Jain Thali ($20.99) prepared without root vegetables, and a Swaminarayan Thali ($21.99) following Swaminarayan dietary guidelines. Our kitchen is 100% vegetarian.

How much does the Thali cost at Vatan?

Thali prices start at $16.99 for the Chaat Thali. Full meal Thalis (Vatan Special, Punjabi, Jain, Swaminarayan) range from $20.99 to $21.99 per person.
