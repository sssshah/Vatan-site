Common Questions

What is the difference between Jain food and Swaminarayan food?

Jain dietary guidelines prohibit all root vegetables — including onion, garlic, potatoes, carrots, and beets — because harvesting them destroys the entire plant. Swaminarayan guidelines prohibit onion and garlic but permit root vegetables like potatoes and carrots. Vatan offers dedicated dishes respecting both traditions.

Are Jain and Swaminarayan items available every day?

Yes. The Jain Thali and Swaminarayan Thali are part of our regular menu, available Tuesday through Sunday during lunch (11 AM – 3 PM) and dinner (4:30 PM – 9:30 PM). East Windsor is closed on Mondays. Call (609) 336-7333 to confirm availability.

Can I order Jain food for pickup or delivery?

Yes. You can place a pickup order online at vatans.com/order.html or call (609) 336-7333 directly. Delivery is also available through Uber Eats, Seamless, and GrubHub.

Is Vatan fully vegetarian?

Yes. Vatan is a 100% vegetarian restaurant — no meat, chicken, fish, or eggs, ever. Our kitchen has never prepared non-vegetarian food.
